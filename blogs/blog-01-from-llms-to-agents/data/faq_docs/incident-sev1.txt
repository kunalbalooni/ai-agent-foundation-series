SEV1 Incident Policy

Definition
A SEV1 incident is a critical outage or security event that causes a complete loss of service, significant data risk, or a major customer impact. SEV1 incidents require immediate, coordinated response.

Initial response timeline
- **Within 5 minutes:** Acknowledge the alert and declare incident in #incidents.
- **Within 15 minutes:** Assign an Incident Commander (IC).
- **Within 30 minutes:** Establish a mitigation plan and status update cadence.

Roles and responsibilities
- Incident Commander (IC): Owns coordination, prioritization, and decisions.
- Communications Lead: Sends external and internal updates.
- Tech Leads: Execute mitigation tasks and provide technical updates.

Status updates
- Internal updates every 15 minutes in #incidents.
- External updates every 30 minutes on the status page.

Escalation rules
- Escalate to on-call engineering manager if mitigation is not found within 45 minutes.
- Escalate to VP Engineering if the incident exceeds 2 hours.

Post‑incident requirements
- Publish a root cause analysis (RCA) within 5 business days.
- Record a list of follow‑up actions and owners.
- Review the incident in the weekly reliability meeting.

FAQ
- Q: Who can declare a SEV1?
  A: On‑call engineers, ICs, or engineering managers.
- Q: Can we deploy a hotfix during SEV1?
  A: Yes, with IC approval and a documented rollback plan.
